Recreations
There's a million things you haven't done...

but just you wait! We'll show you!

Time Square

Known as "The Crossroads of the World," Times Square dazzles with its bright billboards, constant energy, and iconic New Year's Eve celebrations.

Central Park

A vast green oasis in the heart of Manhattan, Central Park offers scenic walking paths, lakes, and cultural landmarks amidst the city's hustle.

The MET Museum

One of the world's largest and most prestigious art museums, the Met houses over two million works spanning 5,000 years of history.

Empire State Building

An Art Deco masterpiece, the Empire State Building offers breathtaking views of New York City from its 86th and 102nd floor observatories.

Statue of Liberty

A universal symbol of freedom, this colossal statue stands on Liberty Island and welcomes visitors arriving by ferry with panoramic views of New York Harbor.

See a broadway show

Experience world-class theater and musicals in the iconic Broadway district, home to some of the most famous stages in the world.

9/11 Memorial & Museum

A solemn tribute to the victims of the September 11 attacks, featuring twin reflecting pools and a museum documenting the events and aftermath.

Brooklyn Bridge

This historic suspension bridge connects Manhattan and Brooklyn, offering stunning skyline views for pedestrians and cyclists alike.

Washington Square Park

A lively Greenwich Village park known for its iconic arch, street performers, and vibrant community atmosphere.
