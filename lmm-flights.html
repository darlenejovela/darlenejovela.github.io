Delta Airlines

When traveling to New York, Delta Air Lines is a strong choice for reliability, comfort, and service. It operates major hubs at both John F. Kennedy International Airport (JFK) and LaGuardia Airport (LGA), offering frequent flights and modern terminals. Delta provides a range of options from Basic Economy to Delta One, along with in-flight entertainment and complimentary messaging, making it a solid option for both business and leisure travelers.

United Airlines

United Airlines primarily serves the New York City area through Newark Liberty International Airport (EWR) in New Jersey. It offers a wide network of domestic and international routes and is especially convenient for travelers heading to Manhattan's west side or northern New Jersey. United features multiple cabin classes, including Economy Plus and Polaris Business Class, with improving terminal facilities and services at Newark.

JetBlue

JetBlue Airways, headquartered in New York, focuses heavily on JFK, with some service at LaGuardia. Known for offering a more comfortable experience at a competitive price, JetBlue provides free Wi-Fi, seat-back entertainment, and more legroom than most in its class. It's especially popular among leisure travelers and those looking for value without sacrificing comfort.

Spirit Airlines

Spirit Airlines is an ultra-low-cost carrier that serves the New York area through LaGuardia (LGA) and occasionally Newark (EWR). Spirit is known for its very low base fares and à la carte pricing model—charging for extras like carry-on bags, seat selection, and even printing boarding passes. It's best suited for travelers focused solely on getting the cheapest fare and who can travel light without many add-ons.
